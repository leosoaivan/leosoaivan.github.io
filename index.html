About Me

Social work and advocacy have always been an important part of the work I do.

Engaging with individuals and families experiencing homelessness, people living with SPMI, and victims of domestic violence has taught me much about the injustices and barriers that a person seeking assistance might face on a daily basis. It has taught me even more about the injustices and barriers I don't have to confront. Luckily, there is no shortage of individuals choosing to work in social services as the field and demand grows. What, then, is lacking and why would I be interested in web development?

My interest in coding began as a hobby in the spring of 2016, spending my free time on the open-source curriculum called The Odin Project. I had no specific career goal in mind, but I quickly found myself addicted to the learning process: agonizing over a challenge, overcoming it, and then finding better solutions than mine. I fell in love with the idea of writing purposeful, maintainable code with potential real-world application. I fell in love with repurposing, refactoring, and even, maybe, testing (sometimes). But I also came to see that the availability of cutting-edge web solutions was seriously lacking among the organizations and individuals most dedicated to solving local, grassroots issues.

As I continue my web-development learning, my hope and endeavor is to continue providing solutions to clients — whether people, communities, organizations, or companies — to help them tackle the issues that matter most to them.
