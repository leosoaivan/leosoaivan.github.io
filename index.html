About Me

Social work and advocacy have always been an important part of the work I do.

Currently, I work as an advocate for victims of domestic violence in Washington, DC. You'll often find me on a 24hr/7d rotation, responding to crises at our local hospitals or police stations. There, I meet with clients to ascertain their immediate safety needs, work out a solution, and schedule them to meet with out advocates for further advocacy. It's good work, if not exhausting.

When not in rotation, after I've caught up on sleep, I spend my free time coding. I began with journey in the spring of 2016 with The Odin Project, an open-source full-stack curriculum. Our stack uses Ruby and Ruby on Rails for the back-end, and I have a soft spot for the ease and clarity that Ruby and Rails bring to the wider programming community. Even so, I quickly became addicted to the learning process, which often includes challenges that are often difficult and unclear. Often times, the cycle included: agonizing over a challenge, finding a solution, and then finding even better solutions (read: learning opportunities).

There is a wealth of Ruby and Rails knowledge out on the internet, so I won't presume to say I'm adding anything meaningful (yet). There have been many moments, however, when I've face interesting and unique enough issues that have caused me problems, even with resources handy. Now, looking back at those moments, I really wish I had taken the time to write something down, especially to reinforce my learning. My blog is part of my 2018 commitment to keep learning and to write better code. It's still in its infancy, but check it out and let me know if you ever find something useful.
