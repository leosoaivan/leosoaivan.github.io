About Me

Social work and advocacy have always been an important part of the work I do.

Engaging with individuals and families experiencing homelessness, people living with severe and persistent mental illnesses, and victims of domestic violence has taught me much about the injustices and barriers that a person seeking assistance might face on a daily basis. It's taught me even more about the injustices and barriers I don't have to confront. Luckily, there is no shortage of individuals choosing to work in social services as the field and demand grow. What, then, is lacking and why would I be interested in web development?

My interest in coding began as a hobby in the spring of 2016, spending my free time on the open-source curriculum called The Odin Project. I had no specific career goal in mind, but I quickly found myself addicted to the learning process: agonizing over a challenge, overcoming it, and then finding better solutions than mine. I fell in love with learning how to write purposeful, maintainable code with potential real-world application. I fell in love with learning how to refactor and repurpose, and even, maybe, how to test (sometimes). But I also came to see that the availability of cutting-edge web solutions was seriously lacking for the organizations and individuals most dedicated to solving relevant local issues.

I believe there's something I can do about that. As I continue my web-development learning, my hope and endeavor is to provide solutions to clients — whether people, communities, organizations, or companies — to help them tackle the issues that matter most to them.

Projects

Most of the projects listed below were completed over the course of my learning. They are imperfect, but they were great fun to make. Feel free to hop on over to my GitHub to see more of my recent work.

My first ever HTML/CSS/JS project, an Etch-a-Sketch which will always hold a special place in my heart. Check out a CodePen here.
